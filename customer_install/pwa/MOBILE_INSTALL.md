# Install Orbi on Your Phone

Orbi works in any web browser, but for the best experience you'll want to
add her to your phone's home screen. It only takes a few seconds and once
it's done, Orbi opens like a real app — full screen, no browser bars, and
she works even when your signal is spotty.

You only have to do this once per device.

---

## On iPhone or iPad (Safari)

1. Open **Safari** and go to your Orbi dashboard URL
   (your installer told you what it is — usually something like
   `http://orbi.local/owner` on your home Wi-Fi, or a secure tunnel link
   if you're away from home).

2. Sign in if you haven't already.

3. Tap the **Share** button at the bottom-center of Safari.
   It looks like a square with an arrow pointing up out of it.

   *(If you don't see the bottom bar, scroll down a tiny bit and it
   re-appears.)*

4. In the menu that pops up, scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**.

5. You'll see a preview with the Orbi icon and the name "Orbi".
   Leave the name as-is (or shorten it if you want) and tap **Add**
   in the top right.

6. Done. Orbi is now on your home screen like any other app. Tap her
   icon any time to open the dashboard.

**Heads up:** Safari is the only browser on iPhone that can install web
apps. Chrome on iPhone can't do it (Apple's rule, not ours).

---

## On Android (Chrome, Edge, Brave, Samsung Internet)

You've got two ways — the easy way and the manual way.

### Easy way

1. Open **Chrome** and go to your Orbi dashboard.

2. Sign in.

3. After a few seconds, Chrome will show a small banner at the bottom
   that says **"Install Orbi"** or a little install icon in the address
   bar (looks like a screen with a down-arrow).

4. Tap it. Confirm. Done.

### Manual way (if you missed the banner)

1. Open the Orbi dashboard.

2. Tap the three-dot menu **⋮** in the top right of Chrome.

3. Tap **Install app** (or **Add to Home screen** on some versions).

4. Confirm. Orbi appears on your home screen.

---

## What "installed" feels like

* Tap the Orbi icon — she opens **full screen**, no address bar, no
  tabs, no Chrome chrome. Looks and feels like a native app.
* She remembers you're signed in, so no password every time.
* If your Wi-Fi drops, you can still open the dashboard and see the
  last data she had. New messages will sync as soon as you reconnect.
* You can get push notifications when a new lead or message comes in
  (enable in **Settings → Notifications** inside Orbi).

---

## Removing Orbi from your home screen

Same as any app:

* **iPhone:** long-press the icon → **Remove App** → **Delete from
  Home Screen** (this just removes the shortcut; your Orbi data is
  safe back on the home server).
* **Android:** long-press → drag to **Remove** or **Uninstall**.

---

## Troubleshooting

* **"I don't see the Add to Home Screen option."**
  You're not in Safari (iPhone) or you're in a private/incognito tab.
  Open a regular tab in Safari/Chrome and try again.

* **"It says 'unsecure site' and won't install."**
  Browsers require HTTPS to install a web app. Use the secure tunnel
  link your installer set up, not the raw `http://` local address,
  when installing from outside your home network.

* **"The icon is a generic blank square."**
  Your installer hasn't dropped the Orbi icon files into place yet —
  let your installer/admin know. Functionality still works fine.

* **"I installed it but it shows yesterday's messages."**
  Pull-to-refresh inside the dashboard, or close and re-open Orbi.
  She'll pull the latest from your home server.
