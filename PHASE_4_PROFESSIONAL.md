# Phase 4 — Professional (after 50 paying customers)

**Goal:** Turn Orbi Web from a one-man operation into a real company. Add hires. Open the App Store presence. Build vertical packs. Consider reseller / white-label.
**Estimated time:** 3-6 months.
**Trigger to start:** 50+ paying customers, monthly recurring revenue ~$7,500+.

---

## Phase 4 deliverables

### 1. Native iOS + Android apps

**Why:** PWA covers 90% of use cases, but App Store presence brings free marketing visibility and "real app" credibility.

**Build:**
- React Native (one codebase, both stores)
- Reuses the API the PWA already talks to — no backend changes
- Wake-word support ("Hey Orbi")
- Background audio for hands-free
- Better push notifications
- Apple Developer account: $99/yr
- Google Play: $25 one-time
- App Store screenshots, descriptions, optimization

**Time:** 6-8 weeks (one focused developer or contractor)

---

### 2. Vertical packs (pre-configured bundles)

**Why:** "Restaurant Pack — $199/mo, everything you need" is easier to sell than "pick from 30 modules."

**Build:**
- **Restaurant Pack** — Chef + Recipes + Inventory + Google Calendar + POS sync (Square)
- **Contractor Pack** — Fleet + Quickbooks + SMS reminders + custom RAG (building codes)
- **Medical/Dental Pack** — Health Knowledge + Google Calendar + SMS reminders + audit log
- **Salon/Spa Pack** — Google Calendar + SMS reminders + custom RAG (color formulas, treatment notes)
- **Legal Pack** — Custom RAG (case files, statutes) + Quickbooks + audit log + privileged mode

Each pack: pre-configured prompts, pre-built workflows, industry-specific FAQ templates, marketing landing page.

**Time:** 1 month (mostly content + landing pages, not new code)

---

### 3. Third (and fourth) brain machines

**Why:** Scale toward 200+ customers without quality degradation.

**Build:**
- Each new brain: $300-500 mini-PC, same setup
- Consider one with a GPU at this stage ($800-1200 for a used 3090 build) — 10x throughput
- Load balancer routes intelligently (which brain has free capacity)
- Geographic redundancy: spread brains across at least 2 different ISPs and 2 different cities

**Time:** Ongoing (1 brain per ~50 customers added)

---

### 4. Hire first support person

**Why:** At 50+ customers, Frank can't do customer support and build features and sell new customers. Something has to give.

**Build:**
- Hire a part-time virtual assistant ($15-25/hr) for tier-1 support
- Build a support runbook (most common issues + how to fix)
- Build a customer-facing knowledge base (reduces ticket volume)
- VA handles 80% of support, escalates 20% to Frank

**Time:** 1-2 weeks to recruit + train

---

### 5. White-label / reseller program

**Why:** Other AI consultants, web developers, marketing agencies could sell Orbi to their clients. They get a cut, you get scale.

**Build:**
- Reseller dashboard: they see their own customers, they bill at whatever markup
- White-label option: replace "Orbi" branding with the reseller's brand
- Revenue share: reseller pays you 70% of customer price, keeps 30%
- Documentation for resellers
- Sales materials for resellers
- API for resellers to provision new customers

**Time:** 1 month

---

### 6. Industry partnerships

**Why:** Get embedded in industry trade associations and software ecosystems.

**Build:**
- Restaurant POS partnerships (Square, Toast) — be in their app store
- Trade association memberships (NRA, NACS, etc.)
- Local Chamber of Commerce demos
- Industry trade show booths (small ones at first)

**Time:** Ongoing networking, not coding

---

### 7. Continuous brain improvement

**Why:** As you collect more data on what queries customers ask, you can fine-tune the brain to be better at exactly what your customer base needs.

**Build:**
- Anonymized query logging (with explicit opt-in)
- Quarterly fine-tune of brain model on real customer query patterns
- A/B testing: roll out new brain to 10% of customers, compare quality
- Keep old brain as fallback

**Time:** Ongoing

---

### 8. Investor / acquisition decisions

**Why:** At this stage, options open up.

**Options:**
- Bootstrap forever — keep all the equity, grow slowly, lifestyle business
- Take seed funding — accelerate, hire 5-10 people, go national
- Sell to a strategic acquirer — Toast, Square, GoDaddy, Wix could all want this
- Franchise the regional model — find a Frank in every city

This is a strategic discussion, not a coding task. Phase 4 success funds the decision.

---

## Phase 4 success metrics

- Customer count 100-200+
- MRR $15,000-$30,000+
- Frank works on what HE wants to work on (not bug-fixing)
- Brand is recognized in target verticals
- The business runs without Frank for at least 2 weeks (vacation test)

---

## Beyond Phase 4

If Phase 4 succeeds, the conversation shifts from "build features" to "build a company." That's a different conversation — strategy, hiring, culture, capital, exit options. That conversation happens when you get there, not now.

For now: get to Phase 1. Then Phase 2. Then Phase 3. The rest takes care of itself if the first three go well.
